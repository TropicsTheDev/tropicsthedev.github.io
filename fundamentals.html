Fundamentals

Guard

Closed Guard

The closed guard is a defensive position in which the grappler on the bottom traps the attacking grappler between his or her legs. By doing so, the attacker’s movement is restricted, allowing the defender to launch sweeps and attacks of their own. A key point of using the closed guard is to try and restrict the attackers moon.

Half Guard

In the half guard, the defending grappler user their legs to trap a single leg of the attacker. While the attacker is granted more freedom of movement, the defender still has access to a wide variety of counter attacks.

Open Guard

Open guard is possibly the most dynamic defensive positions in Jiu Jitsu. The attacking grappler has much more freedom of movement. Because of this, the defender must control distance in order to effective off balance their opponent

Controls

Mount

The mount is an control point in which the attacker sits on their opponent’s upper body. This is a very dominate position that allows the attacker to set up join locks and chokes.

Side Control

In the side control, a grappler lays perpendicular to their opponent’s body, and uses their body weight to restrict their opponent’s movement. This type of controls allows the attacking grappler to block the hips of the grappler on the bottom.

Back Mount

Back mount is a very strong control that is often used to set up chokes. In this control, a grappler puts their legs around the back of their opponent, and uses their arms to control the upper body. There are a variety of ways to use this position, but the main benefit is the ability to control an severely restrict an opponent’s movement.

Submissions

Chokes

A choke or strangle, is an attack that applies pressure to the neck. Some chokes are designed to cut off the oxygen from the opponent. Other chokes restrict blood flow through the neck. Chokeholds, if held long enough, can render an opponent unconscious.

Armlocks

An armlock is any attack designed to hyperextend an opponent’s arm. This is typically done to the joints in the elbows or shoulders. By creating a fulcrum point, leverage is used to apply the correct pressure to the exposed limb.

Leglocks

Leg attacks are becoming more and more popular in modern Jiu Jitsu. Similar to armlocks, leg locks are attacks to the joins of the leg such as the ankles and knees. For beginner’s it’s safe to start with attacks to the ankles. More advanced leg lock’s can be learned as a grappler gains experience, however, these techniques have a higher risk of injury.
